Gambling Addiction Awareness

Take Control of Your Life

Gambling addiction can be overwhelming, but help and support are available. Learn how to take the first step toward recovery.

Understanding

What is Gambling Addiction?

Gambling addiction, also known as compulsive gambling, is a disorder that can lead to significant personal and financial problems. It is characterized by an inability to stop gambling despite negative consequences.

Impact

Effects of Gambling Addiction

Gambling addiction affects not only the individual but also their family, friends, and community. Learn about its mental, emotional, and financial consequences.

Mental Health

Anxiety, depression, and stress are common among those affected.

Financial Problems

Accumulation of debt and financial instability often occur.

Relationship Strain

Gambling addiction can damage relationships with loved ones.

Tips

How to Manage Gambling Urges

Learn effective strategies to help you manage your gambling urges and build a healthier lifestyle.

01

Set Limits

Decide on a budget and time limit before gambling and stick to it.

02

Seek Support

Reach out to support groups or professionals who can help.

03

Avoid Triggers

Identify and avoid situations or environments that encourage gambling.

04

Practice Self-Care

Focus on activities that promote mental and physical well-being.
